Five dimensions
Dimension 1
Distribution economics
Operator lens
"Most African market models underestimate the full commission stack. An agent model that looks like 15% margin on paper often reaches the founder at 4-6% after airtime incentives, aggregator cuts, and churn-driven reacquisition costs."
Founder's stated gross margin *
Distribution model claimed
Has the founder broken down the full margin stack - agent commission, aggregator fee, last-mile cost?
Insufficient information on distribution economics. Do not proceed to IC without the full margin stack broken down by layer. A single gross margin number is not a distribution model.
Dimension 2
Regulatory timing risk
Operator lens
"African governments do not move slowly on digital financial services when political incentives align. Mobile money licensing shifted materially in Senegal (2021) and Ivory Coast (2023) with less than 90 days notice. Most founders model regulatory risk as a general disclaimer, not a specific scenario."
Regulatory dependency *
Has the founder named a specific regulatory risk?
Contingency model if primary regulatory assumption breaks
Insufficient information on regulatory exposure. A founder who cannot name the specific decision that breaks their model has not stress-tested it. Flag this before IC.
Dimension 3
Exit pathway realism
Structural
"Africa's exit-to-investment ratio was 0.13x in 2024. 84% of exits were trade sales. IPOs are effectively closed as a route for most companies. A founder who cannot name three specific acquirers and the rationale for each does not have an exit thesis - they have an exit hope."
Stated exit route *
Has the founder named specific acquirers?
Has the founder had any contact with potential acquirers?
Insufficient information on exit realism. Given Africa's current liquidity environment, an exit thesis without named acquirers and a rationale is a material gap. Do not treat this as a later-stage concern.
Dimension 4
Demand validation
Operator lens
"Africa's demographics are not a market. 400 million people under 25 is a population statistic. The question is whether those people will pay, at what price point, for this specific product, through this specific channel. Survey data and TAM analysis do not answer that question. Paying customers do."
Primary demand evidence *
Is the target price point validated?
What existing behaviour does this product replace - and what does the customer currently pay for it?
Demand validation based on demographics or market size analysis alone is not sufficient. The question is not whether the market exists - it is whether this customer will pay this price through this channel. Do not proceed to IC without at least one concrete paying customer data point.
Dimension 5
Team resilience
Operator lens
"In African markets, the model you invest in is rarely the model that survives. Regulatory shifts, infrastructure failures, currency crises, and partner collapses are not edge cases - they are the operating environment. The team question is not whether they have built before. It is whether they have recovered before."
In-market operating experience *
Has the team navigated a material operational crisis in a comparable market?
Key person dependency risk
Team resilience is under-assessed for this deal. In African markets, key person dependency combined with no documented crisis navigation experience is a material risk - not a later-stage concern. Flag before IC.
Coverage note: This checklist covers 5 of 10 due diligence dimensions. Dimensions not yet covered: currency and FX risk, model origin, unit economics trajectory, infrastructure posture, and cap table structure. These will be added as the benchmark data layer deepens.
