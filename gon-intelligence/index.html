The structural contrast
Senegal

BCEAO Instruction n.001-01-2024 Article 18 prohibits exclusivity clauses in telco-fintech partnerships across WAEMUREG-009. Contractual lock-in is removed by regulation. Distribution depth has to be earned operationally.

Rwanda

Full interoperability achieved between MTN Mobile Money and Airtel Money, creating a single payment infrastructureREG-021. Lock-in is removed by infrastructure. The network itself stopped being the moat.

What this means for a deal Two markets, two mechanisms, same investment consequence: distribution defensibility cannot be modelled as contractual in either. If a deck claims exclusive partnerships as the moat in Senegal, Article 18 makes it unenforceable. If it claims network lock-in in Rwanda, interoperability makes it irrelevant. The moat has to be operational depth: agent liquidity, float management, and merchant density.

Three-test verified. Every quantitative claim passes three tests before it is stored as a benchmark: recency (within 24 months or explicitly flagged), specificity (tied to the exact market and sector, not a continental average), and operator interpretability (I can explain what the figure means for capital deployment). All three pass: stored as a benchmark. Two of three: stored with a named caveat. One or fewer: directional only, never presented as a benchmark.
2
Primary regulatory instruments. Source documents from central banks and sector regulators, read in the original language of publication. Currently covering BCEAO, BEAC, COBAC, ARTCI and ARTP in Francophone markets, and BNR, CBK, CBN and Bank of Ghana in Anglophone markets. Secondary summaries and law firm commentary are logged separately and never counted here.
3
Operator network signals. Observations from practitioners inside the distribution stack that have not been published anywhere. Each carries a confidence tag: primary experience, secondhand, or directional. These are the entries most likely to be wrong and most likely to be useful, which is why the tag is mandatory.
4
Coverage and its limits. Depth is uneven and stated rather than hidden. WAEMU and CEMAC are covered at operator depth: I have worked in these markets and can interpret what a regulation means in practice. East African markets are covered at institutional depth: the regulatory record is complete but not operator-validated to the same standard. Anglophone West Africa is currently the thinnest part of the database and is being actively expanded. Markets shown with a dashed border have fewer than five records and will return limited results.
